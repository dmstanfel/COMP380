Cost

Wearable technology has both physical and social costs. Even though wearable technology has the power to decrease health care cost, it may bring some other unknown costs.

Health care costs are at a all time high. The Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development found that for 30 countries the ratio between health care expenditures and gross domestic product are at a peak. In the diagram below, between the years 2000 to 2015, health care cost increased for most countries.

Radiation

Some patients may be concerned with radiation from smart wearable technologies. There was even a New York Times article warning readers about radiation exposer and cancer risks. But 3 days after the article was released the Editor filed a note saying that "Neither epidemiological nor laboratory studies have found reliable evidence of such risks (cellphone radiation and cancer risk), and there is no widely accepted theory as to how they might arise." and that the article's "headline for the article online — “Could Wearable Computers Be as Harmful as Cigarettes?” — also went too far in suggesting any such comparison."
